Government Agency Redesign

The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) is a government agency that regulates the manufacturing, distribution, and exporting of food, plants, and animals.

Due to the plethora of services that the agency offers to Canadian businesses and citizens, the CFIA faces the challenge of providing a seamless and concise navigation system.

Overview

My intention for this case study was to redesign a website that had a lot of usability issues, so that I may develop an engaging, accessible, and functional user interface.

UX/UI designer throughout the whole design process:
  • Research
  • Definition
  • Ideation
  • Prototyping
  • Testing

Solo UX designer

2 Weeks

Research

In order to identify usability issues on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency website, I conducted heuristic evaluations and annotations for five webpages, although I primarily focused on the homepage.

According to my evaluations, I discovered that the navigation system was unreliable. The menu button sends the user to the navigation of the entire Canadian government website, not the Canadian Food Inspection Agency. There is no dedicated navigation system for the agency, which may cause users to feel lost and frustrated.



Navigation Usability Tests

To further my research, I also conducted navigation usability tests with two users through Zoom. They completed the following tasks:

  • Without clicking or scrolling on anything in the webpage, where do you think we can locate services provided by the Canadian Food Inspection Agency?
  • Using the navigation bar only, try to locate either the Canadian Food Inspection Agency homepage or information that will lead you to the agency homepage.

According to my feedback, users had difficulty determining what was the navigation bar for the website.

Most notably, they were frustrated that they couldn't locate any information or services regarding the Canadian Food Inspection Agency.

The Problem

The existing navigation system on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency website is unreliable and overwhelming.

Through the navigation bar, users cannot easily access services and information regarding the CFIA; they can only view information of the entire Canadian government website.

The Solution

Categorize all services and information from the Canadian Food Inspection Agency in their own navigation system to provide a seamless user-experience.
